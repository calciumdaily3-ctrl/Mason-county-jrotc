About Our Program

The Mason County High School JROTC program, known as the Brigadier General Charles Young JROTC Battalion, is dedicated to developing citizenship, leadership, and personal responsibility in our cadets. Through military-based instruction and hands-on activities, we prepare students for success in college, careers, and life.

In 2015, our battalion was renamed in honor of Brigadier General Charles Young, the legendary Buffalo Soldier who was born into slavery in Mason County in 1864. Brigadier General Young became the third African American graduate of West Point in 1889 and was the first African American to achieve the rank of Colonel in the U.S. Army. He was posthumously promoted to Brigadier General in 2022. Our program proudly carries his legacy forward.

Cadet Activities

🏃

Physical Training

Regular PT sessions focusing on fitness, endurance, and team building activities that challenge cadets physically and mentally.

🎨

Color Guard

Our Color Guard presents the national, state, and school colors at official ceremonies, sporting events, and community functions with precision and dignity.

🎯

Marksmanship

Air rifle training and competitions emphasizing safety, concentration, and precision shooting skills.

🤝

Community Service

Color guards for community events, veteran support activities, and local service projects that build civic responsibility.

🏕️

Leadership Development

Leadership camps and field training exercises that develop outdoor skills and leadership under challenging conditions.

📚

Academic Teams

JROTC Academic Bowl and Leadership Bowl competitions testing knowledge of JROTC curriculum, current events, and leadership principles.
