About Our Program

The Mason County High School JROTC program, known as the Brigadier General Charles Young JROTC Battalion, is dedicated to developing citizenship, leadership, and personal responsibility in our cadets.

In 2015, our battalion was renamed in honor of Brigadier General Charles Young, the legendary Buffalo Soldier who was born into slavery in Mason County in 1864.

🎯

Mission

To motivate young people to be better citizens through leadership education, character development, and community service.

👁️

Vision

Building the next generation of leaders through discipline, integrity, and excellence in all endeavors.

⭐

Army Values

Loyalty • Duty • Respect • Selfless Service • Honor • Integrity • Personal Courage
